Why a paver sealing company built this

JobTap started as an answer to one specific problem: running a one-truck paver sealing company in Jacksonville without losing jobs to a phone that never stops ringing while you're mid-driveway. Every feature in this app exists because it was needed on an actual job site — not because a product team thought it would look good in a demo. If you seal driveways, pool decks, or travertine for a living, this is the trade JobTap was built around first.

Sq-ft pricing that actually holds up

Paver sealing lives and dies by square footage. Quote too low off a curb-side guess and the job eats your margin the moment you're kneeling on a 2,400 sq ft driveway that measured out to 3,100. Quote too high and the customer calls the next guy in the group text. JobTap's quote builder runs on the same sq-ft math a paver sealing business already uses — a rate per square foot, multiplied by a real measurement, not a guess. Set the rate once in your service catalog and every quote after that pulls from the same number, so pricing stays consistent from the first driveway you seal to the five-hundredth.

Quotes your customer can approve without a phone call

A paver sealing quote sent by text goes cold fast if the only way to accept it is calling you back — and you're usually elbow-deep in another job when that call comes in. JobTap's approval portal gives the customer a link they can open, review, and approve on their own time, from the truck line at pickup or the couch after dinner. The second it's approved, it shows up on your side ready to schedule — no double-booking, no forgotten verbal yeses that never made it onto the calendar.

Driveways, pool decks, and travertine aren't the same job

A concrete paver driveway seals differently than a travertine pool deck, and your pricing should say so. JobTap lets you set up separate line items in your service catalog — paver sealing at one rate, travertine sealing at another, pool deck restoration as a flat-rate line when the prep work doesn't scale cleanly with square footage. When you build a quote, you're picking the right line item for the surface in front of you, not doing conversion math in your head between jobs. Pool decks especially tend to carry more prep — coping, expansion joints, stone that's been neglected for years — and a flat-rate option means you're not forcing a job that doesn't fit into a sq-ft box.

On-the-way texts, because these appointments run long

Paver sealing jobs don't always start on the dot — surface prep, weather, and the previous job all push the schedule around. JobTap's on-the-way text fires the moment you mark a job as started, giving the customer a heads-up instead of a driveway full of your gear and no explanation. It's a small thing, but on a trade where you're pulling up in a truck loaded with a sprayer, blower, and drums of sealer, showing up expected instead of showing up unannounced changes how the whole job feels — for a homeowner and for the HOA board watching from three houses down.

The annual reseal cycle, built into the follow-up year

Paver sealing isn't a one-and-done service — pavers need resealing on a cycle, typically every one to three years depending on the sealer and the traffic the surface takes. That's the whole idea behind JobTap's follow-up year: the moment an invoice gets marked paid, a full 365-day sequence starts on its own. A review request goes out on Day 3. A check-in lands at 30. A referral ask at 60. And critically for this trade, the 180-day seasonal touch and the 365-day annual reminder are exactly the checkpoints that turn a one-time driveway seal into a customer who calls you again next spring — or better, one who's already on your calendar because Customers Due flagged them before they had to think about it themselves.

Campaigns that respect who actually said yes

Blasting your whole client list about a spring sealing special is tempting, but it only works if the people receiving it actually consented to marketing texts — and if it lands at a reasonable hour. JobTap's campaign tool builds that in from the start: consent guardrails keep marketing messages off anyone who hasn't opted in, and sends respect quiet hours automatically. For a seasonal trade like paver sealing, that means you can run a real "time to reseal" campaign every spring without babysitting a spreadsheet of who can be texted and who can't.

HOA neighborhoods move in packs

Seal one driveway in an HOA neighborhood and you'll usually get calls from three more houses on the same street within a season — paver sealing sells on word of mouth and visible results more than almost any other trade. JobTap's referral tracking ties a new customer back to whoever sent them your way, so when a neighbor mentions your name at the mailbox, that referral doesn't disappear into a mental note you forget by Friday. Review requests with click tracking work the same way for HOA business: a good-looking, three-day-fresh Google review is often the deciding factor for the next house on the street choosing between you and whoever else knocked on their door.

Questions from paver sealing crews

Does JobTap replace the sealer or equipment I already use?

No. JobTap runs the business side — quotes, jobs, invoices, follow-up. What you spray and how you prep the surface stays entirely up to you.

Can I price travertine differently than concrete pavers?

Yes. Set up separate line items with their own rates in your service catalog — paver sealing, travertine sealing, and flat-rate pool deck restoration can all live side by side.

How does the annual reseal reminder actually work?

It's part of the automatic follow-up year. A reminder fires around the 365-day mark from the paid invoice, and Customers Due shows you who's coming up so you can plan the route before they even call.
